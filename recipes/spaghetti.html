How to Make Spaghetti with Meat Sauce

Restaurant-worthy spaghetti sauce with ground beef is just a few steps away. Here's a brief overview of what you can expect:

Cook Beef and Veggies

Cook ground beef in a saucepan over medium-high heat with garlic, onion, and carrots. Stir until the meat is brown and crumbly. The vegetables should be tender.

Add Tomato Sauce and Season

Stir tomato sauce into the beef mixture. Add oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer until thick and aromatic, then serve over cooked spaghetti noddles.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium sized carrot, diced
  • 1(16oz) can tomato sauce
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Combine ground beef, onion, garlic, and carrot in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Cook and stir until meat is browned and crumbly and vegetables are tender, 5 to 7 minutes. Drain grease.
  3. Stir tomato sauce into the pan. Season with oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer spaghetti sauce for 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
  4. Serve hot over cooked spaghetti noodles and enjoy!
